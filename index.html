I myself am a beginner in Polish, and I decided to start learning using Duolingo, which is great for learning through repetition. However for the most part the grammar is not explained explicitly, unless I went into the comments. I noticed myself going back to the same exercises again and again, so I started looking in the comments, searching for the grammar cases that they mention, looking for other support material and taking notes in a Google Docs. Eventually I made it into a website. It’s been an incredible jump from repetition in Duolingo to actually understanding why I’m saying what I’m saying, so I thought it could help other people as well.

Resources

First let's study the phonology of the Polish language so that we can read the texts in Polish!

I used the help of the iOS app Polish Dictionary & Thesaurus by Farlex, Inc., or the GooglePlay version, which are powered by The Free Dictionary by Farlex. However, for some reason the website version is incomplete and doesn’t have the conjugation of verbs and declension of words is not included. For a desktop or laptop, I recommend using the polish Wiktionary.

You could also just use the polish Wiktionary on your iOS or Android device, although there’s no official apps for it, I have tried a few on iOS and they were as good as the first one I recommended.

Throughout the guide, I also use a few pointers back to the website Mówić po Polsku, Clozemaster, Course of Polish and perhaps a few books, such as “Polish: A Comprehensive Grammar” by Iwona Sadowska, or “Polish Verbs & Essentials of Grammar” by Oscar Swan.

I’ve also been listening to music in Polish on Spotify, making use of the occasional included lyrics. Here are two playlists I’ve made, along with other resources.
